Whole School Dress Code

A high standard of appearance and cleanliness is expected at all times. Clothes should be kept neat and tidy and shoes should be clean and polished. School uniform should be worn correctly - shirts must be tucked in, collar buttons fastened, ties must be of the right length and tied with a sensible knot.

Summer Dress Code (Year 7–11)

Pupils are allowed to wear full school uniform or the following (optional in the second half of the summer term):
(Please note - blazers and pullovers can only be worn as part of the full school uniform. Any pupil who fails to dress correctly in summer dress will be required to wear full school uniform).

Trouser summer uniform

  • No blazer or tie
  • Pullover (optional)
  • White short-sleeve shirt or a long-sleeved shirt with neatly rolled up sleeves
  • Medium grey trousers

Skirt summer uniform

  • No blazer or tie
  • Pullover (optional)
  • White open-neck blouse, short-sleeve shirt or a long-sleeved shirt with neatly rolled up sleeves
  • Regulation Navy Kilt (should be around the knee, no more than 5cm above the middle of the knee and must not be rolled up over the waistband)

Sixth Form

In recognition of their growing maturity and independence, the dress code for the Sixth Form is 'business dress'. This is intended to strike a balance between individuality, establishing a professional working environment and the need to represent the school to the wider community. We hope that Sixth Formers will recognise the importance of taking pride in a smart appearance appropriate to a place of work and of setting an example to younger pupils.

Sixth Form dress should reflect a professional working environment and therefore pupils should look smart at all times.

The list below is not meant to be exhaustive. If in doubt, contact the Head of Sixth Form prior to any purchase to confirm the suitability of any items. 

Business dress

  • A smart skirt, trouser or dress suit, with a blazer, in neutral colours: brown, blue, black or grey. Formal discreet patterns e.g. pinstripe, herringbone or check are permitted. 
  • A smart business shirt / blouse (tucked in) or formal fitted top, with a high neckline, and long or short sleeves (vest tops, tops with a low neckline, crop tops or tops with no sleeves do not conform to policy). 
  • Smart black or brown shoes.

Any business suit from the major department stores that fits the above criteria would normally be deemed appropriate.

Please see the specific guidance below:

  • Skirt, dress or trouser suits are acceptable.
  • Skirts may be worn just above the knee, but no shorter.
  • Plain opaque tights (black) may be worn with skirts or dress suits. 
  • Dark socks should be worn with trouser suits. 
  • Plain jumpers or fitted cardigans may be worn under the blazer in the winter.
  • Top coats for winter wear should be plain and of formal business style.
  • A Sixth Form, House or Colour tie is acceptable.
  • Jewellery should be discreet.
  • Make-up should be minimal and discreet.
  • Nails should not be of an excessive length, but can be varnished.
  • We follow the Halo Code. Long Hair must be securely tied back when health and safety dictates, hair colouring should be natural. 

The following are specifically not permitted:

  • Nose rings: on hygiene and health and safety grounds. 
  • Boots or sharp stilettos: on maintenance and health and safety grounds. 
  • Tattoos: as the legal age to have a tattoo is 18 years. 

Sixth Form summer dress

Sixth Form summer dress is optional in the second half of the summer term.

Pupils are allowed to wear full school uniform or make the following adjustments:

  • Jackets and ties may be left off. If a tie is not worn, the top button - and only the top button - of the shirt must be undone.
  • Shirt sleeves could be rolled up to the elbow.
  • Tights are not allowed.

Additional Dress Guidelines

School Shoes

Pre-School

  • Plain black polishable shoes with velcro straps.

Prep School

  • Plain black polishable shoes with velcro straps or laces.

Year 7–11

  • These must be black and polishable.
  • Plain black, laced,slip-on or with a strap formal shoes with no buckles or decorations.
  • Suitable for all weather conditions.

Sixth Form

  • These must be black, polishable and in keeping with the suits worn in the 6th Form.
  • Plain black, laced or slip-on formal shoes with no buckles or decorations.
  • No boots/ankle boots or sharp stilettos.

Scarves, hats and gloves

Scarves worn with school uniform must be the official School scarf (Year 7-11).  Sixth form pupils may only wear scarves of a plain, tasteful and non-garish colour or the official School scarf. Woolly hats may be worn in very cold weather where the same rules apply, i.e. they should be of a tasteful plain colour and un-emblazoned by logos. Neither hats nor scarves should be worn inside buildings. 

Dress for school visits

School uniform or smart casual dress, or dress suitable for the activities being carried out on the visit. Staff organising the trip will inform all participating of the appropriate clothing to wear and check with Deputy Head if they are in doubt.

Hair

Hair should be of a straightforward style, neat and tidy at all times. During the working day the overall effect should be one that is in keeping with the standards of the School and with the wearing of school uniform.  Therefore should not reflect extremes of fashions; this includes hair no shorter than a grade 2.

The dyeing of hair is not acceptable. Any hair colouring which is deemed unnatural, or where highlights are evident may result in an appropriate sanction. For safety reasons when using machinery in the Design and Technology Department or carrying out a science practical, hair must not come down below the eyebrows. During games it is appropriate that long hair should be tied back.

Jewellery

Valuables are brought to School at each pupil’s own risk.

  • In Years 7-11 girls with pierced ears are allowed to wear one small plain gold, silver or pearl stud in each earlobe.  Earrings are not allowed to be worn in any other part of the ear
  • No other form of facial/ body piercing or tattooing is allowed on view.
  • Sixth Form only: one plain ring or simple signet ring.  A plain chain and bracelets can also be worn

Dining Hall Dress Code

Pupils are not allowed to wear games kit in the dining hall unless specifically told to do so by the PE Department and agreed by the Deputy Head, in which case a clean full tracksuit should be worn. Pupils should not wear topcoats, scarves, hats or gloves while in the dining hall.
